History of the Solar System

The solar system formed about 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant molecular cloud. The sun formed at the center, while the remaining material coalesced to form the planets, moons, asteroids, and other small bodies.

Key Events in Solar System History

  • Formation of the Sun: ~4.6 billion years ago
  • Formation of Earth: ~4.5 billion years ago
  • Appearance of Life on Earth: ~3.5 billion years ago
  • Formation of the Moon: ~4.5 billion years ago
  • Extinction of Dinosaurs: ~65 million years ago

Planetary Characteristics

  1. Mercury: Smallest planet, closest to the Sun.
  2. Venus: Hottest planet with a thick atmosphere.
  3. Earth: Only planet known to support life.
  4. Mars: Known as the Red Planet, potential for past life.
  5. Jupiter: Largest planet with a strong magnetic field.
  6. Saturn: Famous for its prominent ring system.
  7. Uranus: Rotates on its side, has a faint ring system.
  8. Neptune: Farthest known planet, has strong winds.
Dwarf Planet
A celestial body that orbits the sun and has sufficient mass to assume a nearly round shape but has not cleared its orbital path of other debris. Examples include Pluto and Eris.
Kuiper Belt
A region of the solar system beyond Neptune, populated with small icy bodies and dwarf planets.
Asteroid Belt
A region between Mars and Jupiter where most asteroids are found.
